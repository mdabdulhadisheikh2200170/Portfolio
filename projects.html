Research & builds.

Studies in progress and shipped, spanning urban heat, water quality, land-use change, and flood prediction — most of it built on Google Earth Engine, Python, and HEC-RAS.

P.01
Targeting ICACE-2026 · Springer

Urban Heat Island & Surface Urban Heat Island Across Five Bangladeshi Cities

Google Earth Engine · Landsat 8/9 · Mono-window algorithm

Retrieves land surface temperature for Dhaka, Chattogram, Khulna, Sylhet, and Rajshahi using the mono-window algorithm, then correlates LST against NDVI, NDBI, and NDWI to explain which land-cover patterns run hottest, and why. Being developed into a conference paper for ICACE-2026.

Remote Sensing Python Correlation Analysis
P.02
Manuscript in preparation

Spatiotemporal LULC Change & CA-Markov Simulation — Rajshahi Division

Random Forest · Google Earth Engine · 2000–2035

Classifies 24 years of land-use and land-cover change (2000–2024) across Rajshahi Division using a Random Forest classifier in GEE, then projects a 2035 scenario with a CA-Markov model to see where urban growth is headed next.

Land Use Change Random Forest CA-Markov
P.03
In progress

Padma River Water Quality — Source Apportionment & Health Risk

PMF Receptor Modeling · Monte Carlo Simulation

Combines positive matrix factorization (PMF) for source apportionment with a Monte Carlo probabilistic health risk assessment — target hazard quotient, hazard index, and cancer risk — to characterize water-quality risk along the Padma River.

Water Quality PMF THQ / HI / CR
P.04
Research design stage

ML-Based Flood Depth Prediction from HEC-RAS Datasets

HEC-RAS · CNN–LSTM / GRU · Satellite Imagery + DEM

Trains CNN-LSTM/GRU architectures on HEC-RAS-generated flood datasets, satellite imagery, DEM data, and hydrological time series, aiming to approximate flood depth far faster than re-running a full hydraulic simulation each time.

Deep Learning Flood Modeling Remote Sensing

GNN-Based Urban Fluvial Flow Simulation

Graph Neural Networks · Literature Review

Explores graph neural networks as a faster surrogate for urban fluvial flow simulation, with a detailed feasibility assessment and literature-gap analysis aimed at Q1 hydrology journals.
