inspiration

What shapes the work.

The tools, communities, and ideas that keep pulling me back to maps, rivers, and cities — the raw material behind everything else on this site.

instruments

Tools worth building a habit around.

01

Google Earth Engine

A planetary-scale archive of Landsat and Sentinel imagery — the reason a one-person research project can study five cities at once.

02

QGIS & ArcGIS Pro

Where a raster stops being a grid of numbers and starts being a decision — zoning, drainage, exposure.

03

HEC-RAS

The bridge between a river on a map and a river that floods a street. Slow to learn, hard to replace.

04

Python, scikit-learn & TensorFlow

Turns a spreadsheet of past floods and heat readings into a model that can guess at what comes next.

05

AutoCAD, ETABS & SAP2000

The structural half of civil engineering — precision as a daily habit, not an afterthought reserved for exams.

06

DaVinci Resolve

Color grading footage shot on a Sony Xperia 1 Mark II — the one hobby on this list with no raster in sight.

ideas-i-return-to

A few convictions.

Heat, water, and land are not separate problems in Bangladesh — they share the same rivers, the same seasons, and often the same neighborhoods.
Data should survive contact with a planning meeting. A model that can't be explained to the people who'd use it isn't finished yet.
Precision and feeling aren't opposites — the same attention that goes into a raster analysis shows up in how I write, including in Bengali.
people-and-places

Where I keep learning from.

ACES

Alliance of Civil Engineering Students

The department club that first got me teaching AutoCAD to juniors — and taught me a skill isn't really learned until you can explain it.

GEE

Earth Engine community & docs

Endless example scripts, and a steady reminder that most GIS problems have already been half-solved by someone generous.

DL

"100 Days of Deep Learning" (CampusX)

The playlist that took me from EDA and scikit-learn to CNN-LSTM/GRU architectures for flood prediction.

RAS

Open hydrology & HEC-RAS resources

Documentation and forums that make a genuinely difficult tool learnable one model run at a time.
