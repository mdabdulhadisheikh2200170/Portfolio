about

Civil engineer by training, geospatial analyst by habit.

I study how Bangladeshi cities heat up, how rivers carry risk, and how land changes over time — using the same rigor I'd bring to a beam design, applied instead to a raster stack.

I'm an undergraduate Civil Engineering student at Rajshahi University of Engineering & Technology (RUET), 22 Series. Coursework gave me the fundamentals — structures, geotechnics, hydraulics, transportation — but the questions that kept pulling at me were spatial ones: which parts of a city actually run hottest, which stretch of a river carries the most risk, how a floodplain will look in another decade.

That pull led me into QGIS and ArcGIS Pro, then Google Earth Engine and Python, and eventually deep learning — CNN and LSTM/GRU architectures applied to flood prediction. I now sit at the intersection of civil engineering, GIS, remote sensing, and machine learning, mostly working on Bangladesh-specific problems: urban heat islands, river water quality, and land-use change.

I'm looking to take this into graduate study — Water Resources Engineering, Environmental Engineering, or Geospatial Technology — and to keep building tools that turn satellite data into something a planner or engineer can actually act on.
